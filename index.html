Local Government

Council Deadlocked on Compliance Cooperation Resolution

By Margaret Chen | October 15, 2028

A proposed resolution that would limit Millbrook city resources from being used in Federal Compliance Office operations failed to reach a vote Tuesday night after three hours of heated testimony from residents on both sides of the issue.

Council members Garcia and Thompson expressed support for the measure, while members Bradley and Kowalski argued it would put the city at risk of losing federal funding. Mayor Richardson has not indicated which way she would vote.

"This isn't about politics," said resident Ana Morales, who spoke during public comment. "This is about whether my neighbors can walk to the grocery store."

AI Adoption Surges Among Local Small Businesses, But Workers Express Concerns

By David Park | October 15, 2028

Nearly 60% of Millbrook businesses with fewer than 50 employees now use some form of AI assistant, according to a new Chamber of Commerce survey—up from just 12% two years ago. But workers at several local companies say the technology is being used to monitor productivity and flag "anomalies" in ways that feel invasive.

Workers' Collective Votes to Maintain Neutrality on Compliance Issues

By James Morrison | October 14, 2028

In a contentious meeting Saturday, members of Local 847 of the Workers' Collective voted 234-189 to maintain the union's official neutrality on immigration enforcement issues. The decision disappointed some members who had pushed for a solidarity resolution, while others argued the union should focus on workplace issues.

Public Safety

City Watch Chief Addresses Confusion Over Federal Coordination

By Robert Torres | October 13, 2028

Police Chief Diana Oyelaran held a press conference Friday to clarify the department's relationship with federal agencies after social media posts claimed local officers were participating in FCO operations. "Our officers are focused on local public safety," Oyelaran said. "We are not conducting compliance enforcement." She declined to say whether the department shares information with federal agencies.
